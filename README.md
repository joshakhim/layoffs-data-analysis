# 📉 Layoffs Data Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes global layoffs data to identify trends in workforce reductions across companies, industries, and time periods.

The goal is to uncover patterns that can provide insights into economic conditions, industry stability, and company-level workforce decisions.

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset contains records of layoffs across multiple companies and industries.

### Features:

* **company** – Name of the company
* **location** – Company location
* **industry** – Industry sector
* **total_laid_off** – Number of employees laid off
* **percentage_laid_off** – Percentage of workforce laid off
* **date** – Layoff date
* **stage** – Company stage (e.g., Startup, Post-IPO)
* **country** – Country of operation
* **funds_raised_millions** – Funding raised

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Identify companies with the highest layoffs
* Analyze layoffs by industry
* Explore geographic distribution of layoffs
* Examine trends over time
* Investigate relationship between funding and layoffs

---

## 🛠️ Data Cleaning (MySQL)

* Removed duplicates
* Handled missing values
* Standardized date formats
* Cleaned inconsistent text values

---

## 📊 Key Insights

### 1. Company Impact

* Certain companies experienced significantly higher layoffs, indicating restructuring or financial pressure.

### 2. Industry Trends

* Layoffs are concentrated in specific industries, suggesting sector-wide challenges.

### 3. Geographic Distribution

* Some countries show higher layoff frequency, reflecting regional economic conditions.

### 4. Time Trends

* Layoffs increased during certain periods, indicating possible economic downturns.

### 5. Funding vs Layoffs

* Companies with high funding still conducted layoffs, suggesting inefficiencies or strategic restructuring.
